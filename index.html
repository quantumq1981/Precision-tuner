▶ STEP 1 — REFERENCE TONE GENERATOR (Pure Sine Wave)
Click a frequency to generate a pure sine wave internally. The tuner routes the signal directly to its pitch detector for 100% accurate calibration — no microphone or external device required. Tone also plays through your speakers for audible reference.

⎗ STEP 2 — CALIBRATION STATUS & A4 AUTO-CALIBRATE
Select a reference tone above to begin calibration
How it works:
  1. Start the tuner (▶ START button) to activate the microphone.
  2. Click A4 above to generate the reference sine wave through your speakers.
  3. The microphone detects the tone and the status shows ● MICROPHONE MODE with a live deviation reading.
  4. If deviation > 0.5¢, click AUTO-CALIBRATE to adjust the A4 reference.
  5. Replay A4 to confirm the status reads CALIBRATED ✓.
Without the microphone active the panel shows ■ INTERNAL MODE, which verifies signal path integrity only and always reads ~0¢ deviation.
♫ STEP 3 — STRETCH SWEETENING CALIBRATION SWEEP
After establishing the A4 baseline, run a full sweep to verify all 6 string target frequencies (including the active stretch profile offsets) are detected correctly. This confirms the Railsback curve and the selected intonation compensation preset are aligned with your A4 calibration.

If strings show consistent directional offset, use AUTO-CALIBRATE in Step 2 to correct A4, then re-run the sweep. String-specific offsets can be fine-tuned via the Stretch Profile in Settings.
ⓘ Speaker note: The sweep uses internal digital detection — the oscillator feeds directly into the pitch analyser, bypassing the speakers entirely. This means all 6 strings will read GREEN (±0.1¢) confirming the system is computing the correct Railsback-adjusted frequencies for your A4 reference.
